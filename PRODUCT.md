# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Compliance officers, internal auditors, risk analysts, legal teams, financial analysts, consultants, and knowledge workers at banks, insurers, and other regulated enterprises. They work under time pressure, need answers they can defend later, and are evaluated on accuracy and traceability, not speed alone. Secondary audience: C-level executives and admins who need a system-health and ops view, but the primary daily user is the analyst asking questions against indexed regulatory and internal documents.

## Product Purpose

InteliAI is an enterprise AI knowledge platform: a RAG (retrieval-augmented generation) system that lets regulated-industry teams ask natural-language questions against thousands of internal documents, policies, and regulations, and get grounded answers with citations back to source chunks. It replaces manually opening PDFs and running keyword search. Success is a user asking a question and getting an answer they trust enough to act on or cite, quickly, with a visible path back to the source text that produced it.

## Positioning

The only enterprise knowledge platform that shows its work: every answer traces to the exact clause, section, or page it came from, so trust doesn't require faith.

## Brand Personality

Confident, professional, premium, minimal, technical, fast, trustworthy, elegant, quietly modern. The interface should feel like it handles mission-critical information: the emotional register is "I can rely on this," not "look how advanced this is." Tone is quiet confidence, not flashy. Avoid: playful, cartoonish, bright startup gradients, neumorphism, glass-everywhere, consumer-app aesthetics.

## Anti-references

Generic AI-dashboard slop: bright gradient hero-metric cards, glassmorphism as decoration, identical icon+heading+text card grids, purple "AI" clichés, rainbow status colors, uniform framer-motion fade/slide applied to every element regardless of what it's revealing. Also avoid looking like a generic SaaS analytics dashboard — this is a knowledge/search workspace first, not a metrics tool.

## Design Principles

- Show the work: every claim, answer, or stat should have a visible, one-click path to its source — citations are the trust mechanism, not an afterthought.
- The search/ask experience is the hero of the product; everything else (documents, audit, admin) supports it and should recede.
- One primary action per screen; reduce the number of things competing for attention on any given page.
- Real data or nothing: no hardcoded placeholder stats, fake progress bars, or "coming soon" dead ends in the shipped UI.
- Restraint as a feature: near-monochrome base, a single accent used deliberately, motion that communicates state rather than decorates.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA: 4.5:1 body text contrast minimum, full keyboard navigation, visible focus states, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for all motion.
